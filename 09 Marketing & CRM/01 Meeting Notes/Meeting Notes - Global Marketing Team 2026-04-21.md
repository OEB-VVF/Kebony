## Existing Landscape

**CRM**
- Microsoft Dynamics used for the sales part of the CRM
- Only the contact module is in use — the leads module is not being utilised

**Marketing**
- ActiveCampaign is the primary tool for leads, mainly in the form of newsletters
- In the US, leads are pushed to Salesforce
- Outside North America, leads are captured as a list in SharePoint and sales teams manually add them to the CRM
- Website lead inflow is currently in ActiveCampaign but not integrated with the CRM
- Access to ActiveCampaign to be provided to Rishi by Christoph
- Sales teams are not using the CRM appropriately to capture all leads

## Key Issues
1. CRM is used by sales but inconsistently across different geographies
2. Data is stored on SharePoint — not centralised
3. Marketing and CRM are not joined up, making performance measurement very difficult
4. New website development is underway — this has implications for the Odoo implementation; existing systems to remain in the interim
5. Sample requests do not feed into the CRM — they should

## Reporting — No Marketing KPIs Currently in Place
1. Marketing spend per CM
2. Qualified leads
3. Marketing qualified leads
4. Channel spend — currently managed on spreadsheets with geography cuts
5. Reporting tool: Power BI
6. Sample requests currently flow into a data warehouse and a report is generated from there

## Future State
Everything integrated in one CRM with the ability to measure lead performance and quality. Best practices from other companies to be shared.

## Things to Investigate
1. AI feature to suggest how to improve channel effectiveness — e.g. targeting emails to improve click-through rates
2. US uses Shopify to manage sampling, shipping, and logistics — could potentially replicate this using the Odoo e-commerce module
