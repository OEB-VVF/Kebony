3. Priority 1 — Norway CoA and company setup

This section must be executed before any other workstream begins.

Scope

  • Create the three companies in Odoo: BNV, Norway #1, Norway #2.
  • Apply correct localisation package per company: Belgian for BNV, Norwegian for both NO entities.
  • Load statutory CoA per entity. Map every account to the legacy CoA used today (one-to-one or one-to-many, documented).
  • Configure per entity: fiscal year, currency, functional currency, journals, taxes, bank accounts, fiscal positions, payment terms.
  • Set up inter-company configuration so NO → BE flows (H1) and BE → customer flows (H2) can both be modelled.
  • Security groups: at minimum controller, accountant, preparer, read-only.

Deliverables

  • Three companies visible in Odoo staging with correct country + currency
  • CoA loaded and mapped (CSV: legacy_account → odoo_account)
  • Trial run: post a dummy JE in each entity and verify it balances with correct taxes
  • Sign-off from controller on the mapping

Acceptance criteria

  • Every legacy account has exactly one Odoo target account (or an explicit split rule)
  • Every Odoo account has a clear type (asset, liability, equity, income, expense) and tax default
  • CoA differences between NO and BE documented (used later for consolidation mapping)

5. Business-model flip (01-Jul-2026)

The commercial flow changes on the go-live date. Two changes on the same date — treat them as independent workstreams for risk purposes.

H1 (current flow, through 30-Jun)

  1. Norwegian entities hold and sell raw material to BNV under a sales-and-purchase agreement (SPA)
  2. BNV receives the raw material and sells to end customers
  3. Economic effect: Norwegian property value is carried on the NO balance sheet; SPA drives intercompany flow

H2 (new flow, from 01-Jul)

  1. BNV purchases raw material directly
  2. BNV manufactures
  3. BNV sells to end customers
  4. SPA mechanism unwinds; Norwegian inventory reclassifies to Belgian inventory at carrying value

Transition JE (30-Jun close / 01-Jul open)

  • One JE per entity, signed off by Olivier + auditor before posting
  • Reclassifies Norwegian SPA-held raw material to Belgian direct inventory at carrying value
  • Eliminates intercompany margin held on NO balance sheet
  • FX locked at 30-Jun closing rate
  • Preserves lot-level traceability (FSC/PEFC, SPA references)

6. Slow-mover provision — Excel to per-lot

Today the slow-mover provision is a single Excel calculation posted as a lump JE. Going forward, the provision is allocated per lot and carried on the lot itself.

Design choices (to be locked before build)

  • Storage: dedicated field on stock.lot (e.g. x_kebony_slow_mover_provision), OR a separate provision model linked to the lot. Decide before build.
  • Trigger: age-based, turnover-based, or manual — recommendation: age-based (days since last movement) with override
  • Accounting: book provision to a separate provision account (contra class) — do not net against the gross inventory GL account
  • Reversal: when the lot is sold or scrapped, provision releases automatically
  • Reporting: inventory aging with provision column per lot; monthly movement of the provision as a standard report

Transition at 01-Jul

Compute the per-lot provision at 01-Jul and compare to the Excel aggregate. The delta hits P&L as a change in accounting estimate (IAS 8 — prospective). Flag to auditor in writing before go-live.

8.2 Can H1 be reworked after go-live?

Yes, with discipline. Four pre-conditions must be in place before 01-Jul:

  1. Accounting periods for Jan–Jun stay open in Odoo until each month is validated
  2. Every migrated JE carries a batch tag (e.g. x_kebony_migration_batch) so batches can be identified, reversed, and reloaded in bulk
  3. Legacy stays read-only through 31-Dec-2026 as source of truth
  4. Bank reconciliations on migrated transactions deferred until H1 validated

Reload mechanism per month

  1. Freeze period in Odoo (prevent concurrent postings)
  2. Bulk-reverse the prior batch via tag
  3. Re-import corrected batch
  4. Re-run reconciliations (AR / AP / stock / bank)
  5. Re-validate TB against legacy
  6. Unfreeze period

Hard limits

  • Customer-facing artefacts already sent (invoices, statements) — reversal creates credit notes, avoid
  • Reconciliations with external systems (bank, payment processor) — break on reload, must redo
  • Filed VAT periods — reloading JEs inside a filed period requires a corrective VAT return
Target: all H1 rework complete before 31-Oct-2026. After Q3 VAT closes, reworking Q1 becomes expensive. Legacy off-date: 31-Mar-2027, after 2026 audit complete.
