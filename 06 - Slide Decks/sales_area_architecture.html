Why Change the Sales Area Model?

Flat US list cannot serve a global, evolving sales organization
Today
  • 9 flat values — US/Canada only
  • No hierarchy (no parent/child)
  • No manager linked to territory
  • No geography definition — just names
  • Studio field (x_studio_area_of_sales)
  • Europe has no sales areas at all
  • Cannot split or merge territories without data loss
Target
  • Hierarchical tree — any depth
  • Manager (employee) per territory
  • Flexible geography: region, country, or group of countries
  • US + Europe in one model
  • Split/merge without breaking history
  • Auto-linked to analytic accounts
  • Supports budget targets per area
Key constraint: Sales Area must sit on top of Country in the analytics, not replace it. Reorganizing territories (splitting Benelux, merging Nordics) must never break country-level financial reporting.

Two New Models

Flexible geography + hierarchical territory — decoupled by design
kebony.geo.zone

Named geographic zone — can be anything from a sub-country region to a group of countries.

FieldTypeDescription
nameChar"French Switzerland", "Benelux"
codeCharCH-FR, BENELUX
zone_typeSelectionregion / country / country_group
country_idsM2Mres.country — which countries
parent_idM2OSelf-ref grouping
descriptionTextFree text (cantons, depts...)
Why not res.country.group? Odoo's built-in groups are for fiscal positions — system-wide, shared. We need business zones the CEO can reorganize freely.
kebony.sales.area

The actual sales territory. Hierarchical, with manager and analytic link.

FieldTypeDescription
nameChar"Benelux", "North East"
codeCharBENELUX, NORTH-EAST
parent_idM2OParent territory (hierarchy)
child_idsO2MSub-areas

geo_zone_idsM2Mkebony.geo.zone
analytic_account_idM2OAuto-created for leaf nodes
Customers are assigned to leaf areas only. Parents are reporting rollup. Analytic accounts auto-created under the "Sales Area" plan.

Geographic Zones — Flexibility by Design

A zone can be a region within a country, a whole country, or a group of countries
Code Name Type Countries Description
CH-FR French Switzerland region Switzerland Cantons: GE, VD, VS, FR, NE, JU
CH-DE German Switzerland region Switzerland All other cantons
FR-NORD North France region France Île-de-France + northern regions
FR-SUD Rest of France region France Everything south of IDF
DK Denmark country Denmark —
BENELUX Benelux country_group Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg —
NORDICS Nordics country_group Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Finland —
Loose by design. A region like "North France" is defined with a text description, not a formal list of postal codes or administrative boundaries. This keeps configuration simple and in business hands.
Country link preserved. Every zone links to res.country (Many2many). This provides the join path to the Country analytic plan when needed for reconciliation.

How It Flows — From Contact to P&L

Same inheritance chain as today, now with hierarchy + geography
ASSIGNMENT INHERITANCE ANALYTICS res.partner (customer) sale.order Analytic Plan: Sales Area field: x_kebony_sales_area_id ───▶ inherited from partner ───▶ leaf area = analytic account assigned to leaf area only │ ▼ Analytic Plan: Country sale.order.line ───▶ independent (from partner address) │ ▼ Both plans travel together on account.move (invoice) every journal entry line. │ Reorganizing areas never ▼ breaks country reporting. account.move.line (revenue + COGS)
Manager ≠ Sales Rep. The manager_id on the sales area is the territory owner. x_studio_sales_representative on the contact is the deal-level rep. Both are inherited to SO/invoice. Different roles, different fields.
Analytic auto-creation. When a leaf kebony.sales.area is created, the system auto-creates a matching account.analytic.account under the Sales Area plan. No manual config needed.

Territory Splits — Future-proof by Design

Example: CEO decides to split Benelux into Belgium+Lux and Netherlands
Before
Benelux (leaf — 45 customers) Manager: Jan de Vries Geo: BE + NL + LU Analytic: BENELUX
After
Benelux (parent — rollup only) ├── Belgium + Luxembourg (leaf — 30 customers) │ Manager: Sophie Lambert │ Geo: BE + LU │ Analytic: BE-LU (new) └── Netherlands (leaf — 15 customers) Manager: Jan de Vries Geo: NL Analytic: NL (new)
What happens
#Step
1Create 2 child areas under Benelux
2Assign geo zones + managers to children
3Reassign 45 customers to appropriate child
4Benelux becomes parent (rollup only)
5New analytic accounts auto-created for children
6Old BENELUX analytic account stays — history preserved
No data migration. Historical transactions keep the old analytic account. New transactions use the new leaf accounts. Reports show both when rolling up to "Benelux" parent.
Phase 3 bonus: a "Split Area" wizard automates steps 3–6 — presents customer list, lets the manager drag-and-drop to new sub-areas.

Relationship to 5-Plan Analytics

Sales Area is Plan 4 — orthogonal to Country, BU, CM Level, Distributor
Plan Dimension Interaction with Sales Area Source
1. Country 75+ country codes Independent. "French Switzerland" → Country = CH. Splitting territories doesn't touch country data. Partner address
2. BU 9 business units Independent. BU is product/cost driven, not territory driven. Product / GL
3. CM Level P&L waterfall Independent. Margin cascade applies uniformly across all areas. GL account
4. Sales Area Hierarchical territories This model. Leaf nodes = analytic accounts. Roll up via parent hierarchy. Customer contact
5. Distributor Customer category Independent. A distributor can be in any sales area. Partner category
Budget & reforecast: Sales targets can be assigned per leaf area per period. The hierarchy enables top-down target setting (VP sets Europe target) that cascades to sub-areas, or bottom-up rollup from leaf targets. This connects directly to the KPI framework (Commercial family).

Implementation Phases

Models first, then Europe territories, then advanced tooling
Phase 1 — Models + US migration
  • Create kebony.geo.zone model
  • Create kebony.sales.area model
  • Seed 9 US areas (from Studio field)
  • x_kebony_sales_area_id on res.partner
  • Migrate existing x_studio_area_of_sales data
  • Auto-create analytic accounts (leaf nodes)
  • Adapt SO → invoice inheritance chain
US parity. Same functionality as today, now on a proper model. Existing reports, accrual wizard, and dashboards continue working.
Phase 2 — Europe
  • Define geo zones with CEO / Sales VP
  • Build European hierarchy
  • Assign European customers to leaf areas
  • Sales dashboard: hierarchy rollup views
  • Budget targets per area (optional)
Business-driven. IT builds the model; the Sales VP defines the structure. Geo zones are configured in Settings, not code.
Phase 3 — Advanced
  • "Split Area" wizard (auto customer reassignment)
  • Territory map visualization
  • Sales target cascade (top-down / bottom-up)
  • Area performance dashboard
Nice-to-have. Build when the organization is mature enough to use territory-level performance tracking.
