Quality — how the ERP can support you

A proposal from VVF Consulting to the new Kebony Quality Manager
2026-04-22 · Olivier Eberhard · VVF Consulting — ERP Implementation

This note covers Odoo's native quality capabilities and their shortfalls, what Kebony has in place today, and a proposed direction for how we can support the quality function. It is a conversation starter, not a commitment — shape it to fit your priorities.

1. What Odoo gives you out of the box

Odoo has a native Quality app. It integrates with inventory, manufacturing and purchasing, and covers the operational layer of quality control.

CapabilityWhat it does
Quality Control Points (QCP)Rules that generate a quality check whenever a certain event happens — a receipt of a specific product, a given operation in a manufacturing order, a delivery. Measurement types: pass/fail, numeric range within tolerance, photo, text instruction, document capture.
Quality ChecksThe actual check instances on real transactions. Operators complete them on shop-floor / warehouse tablets. An unfilled check blocks the operation.
Quality AlertsNon-conformance records with stages (new → in progress → solved → cancelled), root cause, containment, corrective action, team assignment, attachments.
Quality TeamsRouting and assignment per company, per product category, per operation type.
IoT integrationCalipers, scales, cameras and barcode readers feed measurements directly into the check — no paper re-typing.
Dashboards + SpreadsheetsPass rate, alert count, team workload, trend views; KPIs can be surfaced in the Odoo Spreadsheet module alongside the business dashboards.

Where the native app stops:

  • Customer / supplier claims workflow — needs the Helpdesk app or a custom module
  • Full ISO 9001 document repository (procedures, manuals, training records)
  • Structured CAPA (8D, 5-why) beyond the basic stage model
  • Audit-grade, per-lot document trail tying raw material → finished product — a Kebony-specific need

3. What we propose to layer on top

Five concrete supports, each scoped so you can pick the ones you want to start with.

3.1 Reception quality gate

Deploy Odoo QCPs on every certified-relevant receipt: dimensional check, moisture content, visual condition, FSC/PEFC certificate presence + match, supplier cert validity. Failed check → lot goes to quarantine location, not to available stock. The receipt cannot close until the check is answered.

3.2 Production quality checkpoints

QCPs at the Kebonisation and RDK hand-over points: before loading the autoclave (input mix verification), at unloading (visual + moisture), at RDK receipt (subcontractor claim reconciled against inputs). Ties directly into the existing MO flow and the certification hard-block.

3.3 Customer claims workflow

Build a lightweight claim module on top of Helpdesk (or a dedicated addon, depending on volume). Each claim carries: invoice line, lot(s), root cause, category (dimensional, moisture, cert issue, shipping damage, packaging, other), financial impact, corrective action, stage. Feeds into a monthly claims dashboard.

3.4 Document trail, reception → claim

Extend the FSC/PEFC audit packet so any document (claim, nonconformity, inspection) can be reconstructed from the lot back to the receipt in one click — and forward to the shipping invoice. This is the trail your auditor and your customers expect, and the chain-of-custody module already lays most of the groundwork.

3.5 Quality KPI dashboard

Monthly pack: pass rate per operation, alert count per root cause, claim volume and cost, supplier performance index, FSC/PEFC certified share. Surfaces in the Odoo Spreadsheet module alongside the existing finance dashboards — no external BI needed at this stage.

4. What we need from you to shape this

  • Your view of the two or three most painful quality issues today — these set the order of work
  • Current claim handling: who sees them, what's measured, what's missing
  • Any ISO / regulatory obligations we should design around from the start
  • Your appetite for shop-floor tablets — QCPs are most powerful when operators fill them in real time
