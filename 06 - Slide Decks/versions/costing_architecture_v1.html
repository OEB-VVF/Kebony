Napoleonic → Anglo-Saxon: The Fundamental Switch

From "conversion costs expensed at production" to "full absorption — costs only hit P&L when sold"

Napoleonic (AX Today)
Step Account Where
RM → WIP DR 320 / CR 300, 301 BS → BS
Conversion costs DR 320bis WIP-IC / CR P&L (ICP, IC, DC) BS transit → P&L
PO close: flush WIP DR 604 Écart / CR 320, 320bis P&L immediately
FG at standard DR 330 / CR 714 Var.stocks BS (budget value)
Sale: COGS DR 600 / CR 330 P&L (standard)
Result: Conversion costs hit P&L at production time. FG on BS = standard (budget). COGS = standard. Per-product profitability = impossible without MO drill-down.
Anglo-Saxon (Odoo Target)
Step Account Where
RM → WIP DR 320 / CR 300, 301 BS → BS
Conversion absorbed DR 320 WIP / CR 791 Absorbed BS (stays!)
MO completion: FG DR 330 / CR 320 BS → BS (actual cost)
Sale: COGS DR 600 / CR 330 P&L (actual cost)
Unabsorbed variance 791 balance ≠ actual GL spend P&L at EOM
Result: All production costs stay on BS until sale. FG = real cost. COGS = real cost. Per-product profitability = automatic.

Account Change
320 WIP Scope expanded: now includes conversion costs
320bis WIP-IC REMOVED — merged into 320
330bis FG offset REMOVED — no two-phase
714 Variation stocks REMOVED — no standard revaluation
604 Écart production NOT USED (Option B) — RDK settles to 330/600 at actual

300, 301, 330, 600 Unchanged (but carry actual cost, not standard)
Legal & Transition
Aspect Answer
Belgian GAAP Allowed — KB 30/01/2001, art. 3:15. Policy choice. Disclose in notes.
IFRS alignment Required by IAS 2. Switch aligns statutory ↔ IFRS.
Auditor Positive — prefers full absorption. Will scrutinise WC rates.
Tax impact Higher short-term — costs deferred to BS = higher taxable profit

This Switch is the Prerequisite

Anglo-Saxon full absorption is required for FIFO or Weighted Average to deliver per-product profitability. Without it, conversion costs bypass inventory entirely — no valuation method can fix that.

Level 2 Analytical Decomposition — The Core Improvement

Shared by all options — replaces fixed % with actual MO cost breakdown on every COGS line

What Changes?
Level 1 (AX today) = fixed standard % for all products in a category.
Level 2 (Odoo target) = actual % per product, per lot, traced from the Manufacturing Order.

When goods are sold, the system traces the delivered lot back to its MO. The MO's actual cost breakdown provides the real percentage split, applied as the analytical distribution on the COGS journal entry.

This is implemented regardless of BS valuation choice.
Traceability Chain
Sale Delivery (stock.picking) └─ stock.move.line → lot_id └─ stock.lot → producing MO └─ mrp.production └─ Actual cost breakdown: Wood: 54.3% → CM: Material Chemical: 11.1% → CM: Material Conversion: 16.7% → CM: Conversion Machining: 17.8% → CM: Direct Cost
Fallback: If no producing MO is found (purchased FG, consignment, legacy stock), system falls back to standard % from the planning family.
Before vs. After
Criterion AX (Level 1) Odoo (Level 2)
COGS split method Fixed % Actual % from MO
Granularity Same % for all products Per product, per lot
Drift over time Diverges from reality Always current
Margin variance Cannot isolate driver Wood vs conv vs mach visible
Works with all options? n/a Yes — shared

Cost Component CM Level Source
Wood Material MO component cost (FIFO)
Chemical Material MO component cost (FIFO)
Conversion Conversion MO work centre cost (rate × hours)
Machining Direct Cost MO subcontracting cost (std, then settled)

Key Insight

Level 2 is the minimum improvement regardless of BS valuation choice. Even keeping standard cost on the balance sheet (Option A), COGS analytical distribution uses actual MO percentages instead of fixed ones.

The Three Valuation Methods — What They Really Mean

Standard = blind · Weighted Average = smooth but irreversible · FIFO = traceable truth

Criterion Standard Cost TODAY FIFO (Lot-Based Real Cost) Weighted Average
What you know per invoice Budget number (same for every lot) Real cost of the specific lot shipped Blended average across all lots on hand
Per-product profitability NO — only by product group. Per-product requires opening every MO + manual variance allocation YES — automatic, per lot YES — automatic, per SKU
Lot traceability Lost — all FG at same standard Preserved — lot → MO → cost breakdown Destroyed — averaged away, can never be reconstructed
Reconciliation & audit 4 variance accounts, monthly allocation, auditor must trust your standard Self-reconciling — GL = reality. No permanent variance accounts (RDK settles to actual). Auditor’s preferred method No variance accounts, but values are approximate — never exactly traceable to a specific lot
Cross-market fairness Fair (same standard everywhere) Noisy at single-invoice level Fair (same product = same cost)
Where errors hide Always — standard masks reality until quarterly revision Never — every lot individually visible Sometimes — price swing shifts average for ALL stock
EOM close effort High — 4 variance accounts to allocate, ~200 standards to maintain Low — no variance accounts. RDK settles to inventory/COGS at actual Lowest
Odoo 19 readiness Supported Most mature (lot_valuated) Supported

The Punchline

Standard cost gives you a budget that hides reality. Weighted average gives you a smooth number that destroys traceability forever. FIFO gives you the truth — and from truth, you can always compute any smoothed view you want. You cannot go the other way.

FIFO Self-Corrects Over Volume

Horizon Txns/SKU FIFO vs WA gap
Single invoice 1 lot Visible
Monthly 20–50 Negligible
Quarterly+ 60+ Zero

Nobody makes business decisions on one invoice. At monthly+ level, FIFO and WA produce the same result.

What “FIFO” Really Means at Kebony

Not textbook FIFO — lot-based specific identification with FIFO as the default reservation discipline

How It Works in Practice
Step System Proposes (FIFO) Operations May Override Cost That Flows
MO consumption Oldest lot reserved first Operator picks whichever lot suits the process (dimensions, drying state, proximity) Actual cost of the lot consumed
Sales delivery Oldest lot reserved on pick Warehouse ships whichever lot is operationally practical Actual cost of the lot shipped
Key point: The Odoo product category is set to “FIFO”, but because every lot has its own valuation layer (stock.valuation.layer), the actual cost flow is specific identification (IAS 2.23). FIFO only governs the order in which lots are proposed, not the cost assigned.
In Accounting Terms
Term What It Means at Kebony
FIFO setting Default reservation order: propose oldest lot first
Actual cost flow Specific identification — real lot cost flows to WIP / COGS
Valuation layer One layer per lot, carrying that lot’s actual cost
Why This Is a Feature, Not a Bug

1. Full Traceability

Every COGS entry traces to a specific lot with a specific cost. No averaging, no abstraction. The GL is maximally truthful.

2. Slow-Mover Discipline

FIFO as the default reservation forces visibility on slow movers. The system keeps proposing the oldest lot until someone deals with it — consumes it, downgrades it, or writes it off. Old stock cannot hide.

3. Operational Freedom

When an operator overrides FIFO, they make a conscious decision. The cost recorded is still the real cost of what was actually used. No precision is lost.

IAS 2 Reference

IAS 2.23 allows specific identification for items that are not ordinarily interchangeable. Kebony’s lot-tracked lumber qualifies. FIFO (IAS 2.25) is the reservation heuristic, not the cost formula.

Conversion Variance Tracking & Precision Enhancements

How to track conversion variance in both options · Optional post-go-live improvements

Conversion Variance — Tracking Approach
Conversion costs are absorbed via Work Centre rates (standard €/hour × actual hours). The hours are actual (captured from production). Only the €/hour rate is budgeted (annual cost pool ÷ capacity). The variance arises because actual spend per hour differs from the budgeted rate — this is the only non-actual element in the FIFO valuation layer (~17% of total cost).
Step Description
1 Actual spend — GL accounts track real costs per cost pool (Stacking, Autoclave, Dryer): salaries, energy, maintenance, depreciation
2 Absorbed amount — sum of (WC rate × actual hours) across all MOs in the period, posted via account 791
3 Variance = Actual spend − Absorbed
4 Report in absolute (pragmatic) or divide by budget hours for a rate variance
Option A vs. Option B Treatment

Option A

Conversion variance explicitly posted to 604₂ at each MO completion. Straightforward but per-MO. Sum of 604₂ = total variance.

Option B

No per-MO variance. Tracked at period level: actual cost pool GL vs. sum of absorbed (791). Monthly GL comparison.

Practical note: Not easy to track with full precision. The absolute comparison (actual GL vs absorbed per period) is a pragmatic first step — already a significant improvement over AX.
Post Go-Live — Precision Questions

Monthly WC Rate Revision

What Review conversion cost rates monthly instead of annually. Push revised rates into stock valuation.
Gain Higher accuracy on BS valuation of conversion portion (~17% of total cost). Marginal if production is stable month-to-month.
Cost Monthly close task: recompute rates, update WC config, potential revaluation entries.
Verdict Worth it if conversion costs are volatile (e.g., energy prices). Otherwise annual is sufficient.

Exploding Cost Groups Further

What Split beyond 4 groups. E.g., separate Stacking / Autoclave / Dryer instead of one "Conversion".
Gain Finer margin analysis. But: 1 cost group = 1 margin level. More groups = more complexity for marginal insight.
Cost More WC config, more analytical accounts, more lines in margin report, more complexity in Level 2 logic.
Verdict Diminishing returns beyond 4 groups. Finer splits can be added incrementally post go-live.

Principle

Start with 4 cost groups at go-live (Wood, Chemical, Conversion, Machining). Level 2 actual % already captures the real cost structure per lot. Finer splits are additive and can be phased in without re-architecture.

Option B+ — FIFO + Quarterly SKU Margin Averaging

DECIDED — SteerCo 23 March 2026 · You can always smooth truth. You cannot un-smooth an average.

Layer 1 — The Books (Statutory GL)
FIFO: actual lot cost flows to COGS Every invoice shows the real margin for the lot shipped Full traceability: lot → MO → cost breakdown IAS 2 gold standard — auditor’s preferred method Who uses this: CFO, Controller, Auditor What they see: The truth, lot by lot
Layer 2 — The View (Reporting Query)
For each SKU, for each quarter: Avg margin = Σ(revenue − COGS) / Σ(qty sold) Same product shows same average margin across all markets No lot-to-lot noise No journal entries — it’s a GROUP BY on existing FIFO data Who uses this: CEO, Sales, Commercial team What they see: Fair, smoothed, comparable numbers
Why Option B+ Wins
Question Answer
Does it require journal entries? No — pure reporting layer
Does it require a management ledger? No — just a query
Does it touch the statutory books? No — GL stays at FIFO
How hard is it to build? Trivial — computed column in margin report
When does it become available? After 1 quarter of FIFO data (Q4 2026)
Why Not Weighted Average in the GL Instead?
WA in GL Option B+ (FIFO + report)
Statutory books Averaged (less precise) Actual FIFO
Lot traceability Lost forever Preserved
Cross-market fairness Built-in Via quarterly averaging
Reconciliation Cannot trace to lot Every line traceable
Flexibility Locked into one view Both views
Reversibility Cannot reconstruct FIFO Can always compute WA
Phase 3 (on demand): If the CEO ever needs a full smoothed P&L (not just a margin report), the VVF multi-ledger can produce one with adjustment JEs. Available but probably unnecessary — the quarterly SKU margin report covers 99% of the need.
