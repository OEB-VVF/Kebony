How the Approval Workflow Works

Sequential multi-level approval with amount-based thresholds and rejection loops

The Flow

VENDOR BILL / PO CREATED │ ▼ System matches amount to approval rules (vendor BU + amount threshold) │ ▼ APPROVER 1 receives notification │ ┌────┴────┐ Approve Reject │ │ ▼ ▼ APPROVER 2 Back to draft (if required) (user fixes & resubmits) │ ▼ FULLY APPROVED ──> can be posted / confirmed

Key Design Principles

Sequential, not parallel

Approvers are ordered (sequence 10, 20, 30...). Each must approve before the next is notified. No skipping.

Amount-based thresholds

Different rules trigger depending on invoice/PO total. Small amounts may need only 1 approver; large amounts need 2-3 levels.

Vendor Business Units

Vendors are classified into BUs (e.g. Logistics, Raw Materials, Services). Each BU has its own approval chain and thresholds.

Rejection = back to start

Any rejection resets the document to draft. The submitter fixes the issue and re-submits, restarting the full approval chain.

The kebony.approval Model

One record per document needing approval

Tracks the full approval lifecycle:

  • State: pending / approved / rejected
  • Current approver: who's next in the sequence
  • Approval log: who approved/rejected, when, with comments
  • Amount: total amount that triggered the rule
  • Rule matched: which BU + threshold applied

Notifications & Audit

  • Email notification sent to each approver when it's their turn
  • Chatter message on the document for every approve/reject action
  • Full audit trail — who, when, what, comments
  • Dashboard: "My Approvals" shows pending items per user
Security: Only designated approvers can approve. The "Approve" button is invisible to non-approvers. Users cannot approve their own requests.

Why This Matters

From informal email approval to auditable, enforced workflow

Today (without)

  • Approval by email or verbal OK
  • No audit trail
  • Anyone can post a vendor bill
  • POs confirmed without budget check
  • No visibility on pending approvals
  • Risk of unauthorized spend

Tomorrow (with)

  • Approval enforced in the system
  • Full audit trail with timestamps
  • Posting blocked until approved
  • Amount-based escalation
  • "My Approvals" dashboard
  • Rejection loop with comments

Key Benefits

  • Compliance: Segregation of duties
  • Control: No spend without approval
  • Visibility: Real-time approval status
  • Speed: No more chasing emails
  • Audit: Instant trail for external auditors
  • Scalable: Add BUs, thresholds, approvers as org grows
