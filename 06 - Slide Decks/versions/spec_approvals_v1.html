Overview — What & Why

A unified approval layer for financial documents

Business Need

  • All Vendor Bills and Purchase Orders must be approved before posting/confirmation
  • Approval chain driven by the Business Unit assigned to each vendor
  • Optional amount-based thresholds trigger additional approvers
  • Full audit trail with email notifications at each step

Scope

  • account.move — Vendor Bills
  • purchase.order — Purchase Orders
  • New kebony.approval configuration model
  • Centralized approval list view & follow-up widget

Key Principles

Vendor-Driven Routing

Each vendor has a Business Unit. The BU determines which approval rule applies.

Sequential Approval

Approver 1 must sign off before Approver 2 is notified. No parallel approval.

Blocking Workflow

Documents cannot be posted/confirmed until all required approvers have approved.

Rejection Loop

Rejected documents return to the responsible user for corrections, then re-enter the approval chain.

Amount-Based Extra Approver

Approver extra

  • Based on amount — checkbox to enable threshold
  • Min — minimum amount (currency) triggering extra approval
  • Amount approver — user who must approve above threshold

Min & Amount approver fields only displayed if "Based on amount" is checked.

Model Selection Logic

Select between: ├─ account.move (Vendor Bill approvals) │ └─ for specific vendor │ Company / BU combo │ └─ purchase.order (PO approvals) └─ for specific vendor Company / BU combo Select any other model : └─ Custom fields

Validation Rules

Vendor Bills (account.move)

Always 2 validators: BU Head + another BU unit head (e.g., Finance)

Purchase Orders (purchase.order)

  • 1 validator if one → PO responsible only
  • 2 validators if no → BU Head + another BU head (e.g., Finance)

Workflow — Vendor Bills

account.move approval lifecycle

Step-by-Step Flow

Step 1 — User Confirms the Bill

  • User opens Vendor Bill in Draft status
  • Clicks Confirm
  • System sends email notification to Approver 1
  • Status → Waiting for Approval
↓

Step 2 — Approver Reviews

  • Approver sees Approve and Reject buttons
  • Approve → if Approver 2 exists, notification sent to next approver
  • Reject → email sent to Bill responsible to make changes
↓

Step 3 — Final Approval & Posting

  • Final approver clicks Approve → status changes to Approved
  • Users from "Accounting Department" group can then post the invoice

Status Values

Status Meaning
Draft Initial state — editable
Waiting for Approval Confirmed, pending approver action
Approved All approvers signed off
Approval Rejected Returned for changes
Posted Standard Odoo posted state

Approval Flow Diagram

User clicks Confirm │ ▼ Approver 1 receives notification ├── Approve ──▶ Approver 2 notified │ ├── Approve ──▶ ✓ Approved │ └── Reject ──▶ Back to responsible └── Reject ──▶ Back to responsible After approval: Accounting Dept ──▶ Post Invoice
Rule: All vendor bills must be approved by their respective BU head. Only Accounting Department users can post after approval.

Invoice Validation — PO Deviation Control

Strengthening 3-way matching with threshold-based deviation checks

Why Standard 3-Way Matching Falls Short

Odoo Native Behaviour

Standard 3-way matching (PO → Receipt → Invoice) only checks that a receipt exists. It does not flag price or quantity deviations between the PO and the vendor bill.

The Risk

  • Vendor invoices higher unit prices than agreed on PO
  • Invoiced quantities exceed received quantities
  • Additional charges or fees not on the original PO
  • All pass through without detection under standard matching

Proposed Enhancement

Deviation Check Before Approval

When a vendor bill is confirmed, the system compares each bill line against the linked PO line and flags deviations exceeding configurable thresholds.

Threshold Configuration

Check Comparison Threshold
Unit Price Bill line price vs PO line price % tolerance (e.g., 5%)
Quantity Bill qty vs received qty % tolerance (e.g., 10%)
Line Total Bill line amount vs PO line amount Absolute & % tolerance
Unmatched Lines Bill lines with no PO reference Always flagged

Workflow Integration

Bill Confirmed │ ▼ Deviation Check ├── Within tolerance ──▶ Normal approval flow │ └── Exceeds tolerance ──▶ Flagged for review └── Deviation details visible in Approvals tab └── Approver must Accept or Reject deviation
Key rule: Bills with deviations above threshold cannot be approved without explicit acknowledgement of the deviation by the approver.

Notifications & Security

Email workflow, access rights, and audit trail

Notification System

On Confirm (User → Approver 1)

Email sent to Approver 1 with document link. Chatter message logged.

On Approve (Approver 1 → Approver 2)

If first approver clicks "Approve" and Approver 2 exists, an email/notification is sent to the next approver.

On Reject (Approver → Responsible)

An email/notification is sent back to the Bill/PO responsible so they can make changes and re-submit.

On Final Approval

Status changes to "Approved". Accounting / PO Department users can then post or confirm.

Security & Access Control

Role Can Do
Bill/PO Creator Create, edit draft, click Confirm
Approver (current) See Approve/Reject buttons (only when it's their turn)
Approver (not current) View only — no action buttons
Accounting Department Post approved Vendor Bills
PO Department Confirm approved Purchase Orders

Audit Trail

  • All approval actions logged with timestamp + user
  • Chatter messages on each state transition
  • Rejection reason tracked for accountability
  • Full history visible in the Approvals tab
Key design: Approvers only see Approve/Reject buttons when it is specifically their turn in the chain. This prevents out-of-order approvals.

Budget Control Module

Linked workstream — developed by VVF in sync with the approval workflow

Why a Dedicated Module

Odoo Native Limitations

Odoo’s built-in budget features are basic (analytic-account level only) and do not integrate with manufacturing cost allocation or inventory valuation standards.

Accounting Standards Requirement

Under IAS 2 / ASC 330, manufacturing indirect costs (overhead) must be capitalized into inventory. Budget control and cost allocation are two sides of the same coin — they must use the same cost pools and rates.

Scope — What the Module Covers

  • Budget definition per cost centre / department / BU
  • Budget consumption tracking against POs and vendor bills
  • Threshold alerts when spend approaches budget limits
  • Integration with approval workflow — over-budget POs/bills flagged during approval

Link to Manufacturing Indirect Costs

Budget Pools ├── Direct costs (raw material, chemicals) │ └── tracked via PO/Bill │ └── Indirect costs (overhead, utilities, maint.) ├── Budget module tracks spend └── Same rates feed inventory valuation (IAS 2 / ASC 330)
One source of truth: Indirect cost rates defined in the budget module are the same rates used for manufacturing overhead absorption into inventory. No duplication, no drift.

Responsibility Split

Component Developed By
Approval workflow (this deck) Odoo (custom dev)
PO deviation checks Odoo (custom dev)
Budget control module VVF Consulting
Manufacturing cost allocation VVF Consulting
Sequencing: The budget module ships alongside or shortly after the approval workflow, so that budget checks can integrate into the approval chain from day one.
