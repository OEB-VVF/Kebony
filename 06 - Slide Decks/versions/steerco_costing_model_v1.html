Weighted Average

Ruled out

  • Smooth cost, no lot noise
  • Destroys lot traceability forever
  • Cannot trace cost back to MO
  • Prevents specific lot analysis
What "FIFO" really means at Kebony: Each lot carries its own actual production cost. FIFO is the default reservation order (oldest lot proposed first), not a cost averaging method. Operators can override and pick any lot. The cost follows the specific lot sold — true specific identification per IAS 2.23.

COGS Decomposition — 5 Cost Components

Every invoice line carries a full cost breakdown for margin analysis
Component Source Method
Wood (raw material) PO price + landed cost Actual per lot (FIFO layer)
Chemicals Ready-mix MO cost FIFO per mix batch
Conversion Std rate × actual hours Stacking + Autoclave + Dryer
Machining (RDK) Two-step: std at MO, actual at bill Settles back to FG/COGS
Packaging BOM component FIFO per packaging lot
Anglo-Saxon model: All 5 components stay on the balance sheet (inventory) until the product is sold. COGS hits P&L only at invoice time. This replaces the current Napoleonic model where conversion costs are expensed at production.

US Entity — Additional Cost Layers

On top of product COGS, four accrual-based costs are computed per invoice line:

Accrual Rate Trigger
Marketing 4% / 1.5% Distributor only
Sales Rep Variable If rep assigned
Royalties 5% Wood, no consignment
Management Fees 14% / 5% No consignment

Full margin = Revenue - COGS - Freight - Accruals - Adjustments

Visible on every posted invoice. Recomputable on demand.
