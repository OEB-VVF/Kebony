1. Why analytical accounting exists

The GL (chart of accounts) is deliberately simple. One revenue account, one COGS account, one payroll account. Without analytics, you can see what happened in financial terms but you cannot see where, for whom, which product, or at which margin level.

Analytics add tags — multi-dimensional labels — on every journal entry line. Reporting then pivots on those tags. The GL stays clean; the reporting is rich.

Analogy: the GL is the cash register tape. Analytics are the product category / store region / customer segment stickers. The tape alone tells you revenue; the stickers tell you why.

The five questions analytics answer

  1. What type of product did we sell or produce? → Product Group
  2. Where in the P&L waterfall does this line sit? (revenue, direct COGS, overhead, marketing…) → Cost Group / CM Level
  3. Which country / territory did we sell into? → Country
  4. Which department / division owns the cost or revenue? → Business Unit (BU)
  5. Which US sales area (for Kebony Inc. only)? → Sales Area

Stage 2 — end of month (wizards + allocations)

  1. Run EOM accruals (US only — marketing, royalties, mgmt fees, sales rep, freight)
  2. Compute unabsorbed production costs (BNV) = actual Operations cost − absorbed via work-centre rates
  3. Allocate to CM levels (unabsorbed production → COGS-I; overhead → CORP / SALES / MKT)
  4. Distribute country (BNV) — costs tagged COM are split across countries by m³ sold
  5. Validate and post the allocation journal entries

7. A worked example — one sale, all analytics

BNV sells a Scots Pine terrace pack to a customer in Germany. Here's how the journal entries get tagged.

Customer invoice (BNV) — revenue line
Account: 700000 (Revenue)
Debit: 0 Credit: 10 000 EUR
Analytic distribution: Product Group = KSP (Scots Pine) Country = DEU (Germany) BU = 52 (Sales International) CM Level = REV
COGS journal entry (BNV) — materials portion
Account: 600000 (COGS — materials)
Debit: 4 500 EUR Credit: 0
Analytic distribution: Product Group = KSP Country = DEU (inherited from SO) BU = 30 (Operations) CM Level = COGS-D
COGS journal entry (BNV) — conversion portion (via BoM decomposition)
Account: 600500 (WC absorption)
Debit: 1 800 EUR Credit: 0
Analytic distribution: Product Group = KSP Country = DEU BU = 30 (Operations) CM Level = COGS-I

Resulting margin view for this sale:

  • Revenue: 10 000 → tagged DEU, KSP, REV
  • COGS-D: 4 500 → GM I = 5 500
  • COGS-I: 1 800 → GM II = 3 700
  • Reports pivot instantly by Country (DEU), Product Group (KSP), BU (52/30) — no GL-account gymnastics required.

9. End-of-month — what happens

  1. All invoices posted, all MOs completed (BNV), all vendor bills posted.
  2. US: Accountant runs the EOM accrual wizard (marketing, royalties, mgmt fees, sales rep, freight). Accruals post with full analytic distribution.
  3. BNV: Accountant computes unabsorbed production costs (actual Operations cost − absorbed via WC rates). Difference allocated to COGS-I.
  4. BNV: Costs tagged Country=COM are distributed to real countries using m³ sold as the key.
  5. A monthly COGS reclassification JE allocates unallocated COGS to sales areas (US), using the margin report's grouping.
  6. Controller reviews the allocation journal entries and posts them.
  7. Reports produce the full margin waterfall per Product Group × Country × BU.

10. FAQ

Q. I don't see an analytic field on my journal entry line. What do I do?

Enable analytic distribution on the line. In Odoo 19 it opens a dropdown where you can add tags across all five plans. If the field is greyed out, check that the GL account is a P&L account (6* or 7*).

Q. Can one line have two tags on the same plan? (e.g. 50% USA, 50% CAN)

Yes. Analytic distribution is a percentage split across accounts within a plan. Use it sparingly — it's there for situations where a single transaction genuinely spans multiple buckets.

Q. What if the system auto-fills the wrong BU?

Override it on the specific line. Defaults are hints, not rules. A common override is lines that belong to Marketing (BU 60) on an invoice whose default is Sales International (BU 52).

Q. I posted a JE last month without analytics. Am I stuck?

No. The Backfill Analytics wizard (Accounting Hub menu) updates posted JEs retroactively. It merges new analytics into the existing distribution — it doesn't overwrite lines that already have a Product Group or BU. Run it by company and by date range.

Q. Why is the COGS split different for BNV and INC?

BNV converts raw wood into finished goods — there's real direct (materials) and indirect (conversion) cost. INC buys finished goods from BNV and resells — no conversion, so all of its COGS is "direct" from its own books. At group consolidation, the direct-vs-indirect split is pulled from BNV's analytics.

Q. Why tag countries on production costs only at EOM?

At the time of production, we don't yet know which country will buy the output. Instead of guessing, we tag the cost COM (common / unallocated) and distribute at EOM using m³ sold by country as the key.

Q. When BNV sells to INC (intercompany), which country wins?

USA — decided. The Canada gap (INC reselling some of those goods into Canada) is corrected at EOM by reallocating BNV's IC margin proportional to INC's m³ sold by country.

Q. A customer moves from North East to South East. Do I have to reload history?

No. Change the contact's Sales Area field going forward. Historical invoices stay tagged with the old area — that's the correct representation of what happened at the time.

  • Country distribution key for all allocations is m³ sold. Always.
