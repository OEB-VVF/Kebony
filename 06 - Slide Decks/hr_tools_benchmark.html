HR Tools Benchmark

Odoo 19 HR Suite vs. Officient — Scope, Effort & Budget
Prepared for Kebony HR Director & CEO — April 2026 | Confidential

Executive Summary

Kebony currently runs HR on Officient — a Belgian HR SaaS covering employee records, time tracking, leaves, documents, and a native export to SD Worx (outsourced payroll). The question is whether Odoo 19's HR suite, already paid for, can replace Officient without taking on payroll.

Short answer: Odoo covers 80–90% of the scope out of the box, and is stronger than Officient on anything that touches the ERP (expenses, project timesheets, fleet, skills, appraisal, training registry with mandatory-training expiry tracking). The single real risk is the SD Worx export — Officient has it natively, Odoo does not. Building a clean monthly export is a focused, well-bounded dev job, not a moonshot.

Bottom line: Migration is technically feasible at a fixed one-month package of €20K, including the Mandatory Training Matrix dashboard. The decision is operational, not technical: does the HR Director want to consolidate on the ERP, or keep a dedicated HR SaaS?

⏱️ Time Tracking — The Critical Topic

What Odoo 19 Can Do

  • Attendances — clock in/out via web, mobile app, kiosk, RFID badge, or PIN
  • Timesheets — project/task-based hours (useful for manufacturing + service)
  • Planning — shift planning, rotating schedules (relevant for production)
  • Overtime computation, break auto-deduction, rounding rules
  • Manager approval workflow
  • Reports by employee, department, project, cost center
  • Geofencing on mobile clock-in

Officient Strengths

  • Designed explicitly for Belgian SME working-time rules
  • Native SD Worx export — this is the big one
  • Vacation/sick leave auto-feeds the timesheet
⚠️ The SD Worx export challenge. Officient ships a pre-built connector that sends monthly time + leave data to SD Worx in their required format. Odoo has no native SD Worx connector for time data — its BE payroll module exists but is not on our scope.

What this means concretely: to leave Officient, we need a custom monthly export from Odoo → SD Worx. SD Worx accepts CSV/Excel imports for variable elements (hours worked, overtime, absences, allowances). The format is documented and stable.

Effort estimate: 4–7 days of development + 2 days of parallel-run testing. Not trivial, but scoped and linear.
~ Odoo's time-tracking engine is more powerful than Officient's. The gap is the SD Worx handoff, which is a one-time integration cost.

💸 Payroll — Explicitly Out of Scope

Why we keep SD Worx

  • BE payroll is legally complex — social security, sectoral agreements (CP), meal vouchers, eco-cheques, holiday pay provisions
  • SD Worx handles the filings, declarations, slip generation — that expertise is not worth rebuilding
  • Odoo BE payroll exists but carries real compliance risk without a specialist payroll team on-site

What Odoo must deliver to SD Worx (monthly)

  • Hours worked per employee (regular, overtime, Sunday, holiday)
  • Absences by type (holiday, sick, etc.)
  • Variable elements: meal vouchers, expenses, commissions, bonuses
  • New hires / departures / contract changes
~ Not a module question — a file-export question. 1 custom "SD Worx Export" wizard replaces Officient's native connector.

🎓 Training Registry & Mandatory Training Tracking

HR Director explicit ask: per employee, track what training (past + future), when, and flag mandatory training that is missing or expiring — e.g. safety, forklift, quality, GDPR, first aid.

What Odoo 19 Can Do

  • Skills + Certifications on the employee — each certification carries an acquisition date and expiry date; expired certs flag automatically
  • eLearning (Slides) — host internal training courses, track completion per employee, issue certificates
  • Surveys — quiz/exam after training, pass/fail recorded against the employee
  • Appraisal — training needs captured during review cycles, feed into training plan
  • Calendar + Events — schedule upcoming classroom/external training sessions, register attendees
  • Documents — attach certificate PDFs (external training) to the employee file
  • Self-service: employee sees own training history + upcoming obligations on their portal

🎯 Recommendation for Kebony

Phase 0 — Decide, don't migrate yet

  • Confirm with HR Director that consolidation on ERP is the operating goal — this is a people-process question before it's a software one
  • Pull the exact SD Worx file spec (format, cadence, fields) — this is the single technical unknown and de-risks the whole budget
  • Audit Officient's contract/document templates to scope the re-creation effort

Phase 1 — One-month implementation package

  • Week 1 — configuration + data migration + SD Worx spec lock-in
  • Week 2 — SD Worx export build + training registry catalog + Mandatory Training Matrix dashboard
  • Week 3 — pilot on one department (e.g. Kallo production, ~15 people), parallel run with Officient
  • Week 4 — reconcile SD Worx output, extend to all BE employees, training + handover
  • Budget: €20K fixed (1,000 €/day blended × 20 days, discounted from 1,100 €/day standard)

Phase 2 — Decommission Officient

  • Keep Officient live for one full SD Worx cycle after go-live (safety net)
  • Shut down Officient at the next month-end; Odoo takes over fully
  • Budget: inside Phase 1 envelope

What NOT to Do

  • Don't migrate and decommission Officient in the same month — always keep it live during one full SD Worx cycle
  • Don't touch Odoo's BE payroll module — the scope boundary with SD Worx is the whole point of this project
  • This is not a "cost saving" project — the real win is ERP integration (skills, projects, planning, cost centers, training compliance). The license saving just covers the migration cost.
