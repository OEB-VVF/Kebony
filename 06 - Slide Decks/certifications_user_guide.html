1. Why we track certifications

Kebony sells certified wood. Customers pay for the claim, and both FSC and PEFC require us to reconstruct — for any outbound shipment — which certified input lots came in, how they were transformed, and which sales document carried the claim out. Retention: 5 years.

Beyond chain-of-custody, US building codes require a separate approval: ICC-ES. This is not about the wood's provenance; it's about whether the finished product is code-legal for use in American buildings.

Both must be in place before the invoice goes out. This guide covers what users need to know, with pointers into the canonical architecture document when you need the deeper detail.

2. The two certification axes

Live FSC / PEFC — chain of custody

FSC = Forest Stewardship Council · PEFC = Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification. Both are required in practice at Kebony — never "just FSC".

Carrier: the lot. Every incoming lot is tagged with scheme + claim + supplier certificate code + evidence.

Current state: "FSC Mix 70%" and "PEFC Certified" — Kebony operates under the percentage method, transitioning toward 100% certified input.

New axis ICC-ES — US building-code compliance

ICC-ES = ICC Evaluation Service. Issues ESRs (Evaluation Service Reports) — numbered approvals that confirm a product is code-legal under US building codes (IBC, IRC, IFC) for structural use, fire rating, decking load, cladding attachment, etc.

Carrier: the product (SKU / product template). Not the lot.

Market scope: US only. Irrelevant for European sales.

4. Manufacturing — Kebonisation and RDK

HARD BLOCK — no mixing of different certifications in the same MO.
Applies to both Kebonisation (in-house autoclave) and RDK (subcontract). All consumed input lots must share the same scheme and claim. Enforced in Python at MO confirm and done — no "override" flag.

Kebonisation MO

  • At action_confirm: system reads the claim on every consumed lot. If any differ, MO cannot be confirmed.
  • At action_done: output lot inherits the uniform input claim.
  • If the user needs to run a different claim: create a separate MO.

RDK MO (subcontract)

  • Primary control is contractual: the subcontract includes explicit no-mixing clauses and evidence requirements.
  • Technical layer: same input-side block as Kebonisation at action_confirm.
  • When output is received from the subcontractor, the claim they declare must match the claim their inputs supported. Mismatch → output is quarantined and a cert.nonconformity is logged automatically.
Non-Kebonisation / non-RDK MO types (repacks, grade-downs) keep the earlier policy: warn or block, configurable per product group. Only these two MO types have the hard block.

5. Sales orders — the certification toggle

New toggle on the sales order line, mirroring the existing minimum quantity guarantee toggle.

Field on SO lineBehaviour
Certification required (toggle)Switches on the certification gate for this line
Scheme required (FSC / PEFC)Which scheme the customer needs
Claim requiredE.g. "FSC Mix 70%", "FSC 100%", "PEFC Certified"

Downstream effects

  1. Planning / reservation: only lots matching the required scheme + claim are candidates for reservation on this line. If the product is US-bound, ICC-ES requirement is also checked (§8).
  2. Delivery control: the picking cannot be validated if any reserved lot fails the match. Error message is explicit — the user sees which lot fails and why.
  3. RDK fulfilment: if the line is fulfilled through an RDK subcontract, the certification requirement propagates into the subcontract PO, and is re-checked at receipt.

6. RDK subcontract specifics

RDK certification is enforced in three places:

  1. Contract — no-mixing clauses, supplier certificate evidence, claim declaration format.
  2. Inputs sent — same block as Kebonisation: all consumed input lots must share the same claim.
  3. Output received — the claim declared by the subcontractor on their delivery document must match what the inputs supported. Mismatch = quarantine + nonconformity record. Ops must clear the nonconformity before the goods can be used.

Subcontractor's own certificate is tracked in the partner.certificate register alongside customers and suppliers.

7. Invoice as proof of certification

The invoice is the customer-facing proof of certification. Every certified line carries:

  • Scheme — FSC or PEFC
  • Claim class — e.g. "FSC Mix 70%", "PEFC Certified", "FSC 100%"
  • Certificate number — company-level (e.g. FSC-Cxxxxxx) or per-lot where the scheme demands it
  • Where applicable: ESR number for US-bound product (see §8)

The invoice → delivery → lot → claim chain must remain intact for the five-year retention horizon. This is the auditor's walking path: they pick an invoice and ask us to show the evidence back to the vendor document.

8. ICC-ES — US building-code certification

A different animal from FSC / PEFC.

What ICC-ES is

  • ICC-ES — ICC Evaluation Service (a technical arm of the International Code Council)
  • Issues Evaluation Service Reports (ESRs) — numbered reports approving a product for use under IBC / IRC / IFC
  • Covers: structural use, fire rating, decking / stair loads, cladding attachment, fasteners, etc.
  • Architects, general contractors and building inspectors look up the ESR number to confirm a product is code-legal.

How it lives in Odoo

On the product.template (not on the lot):

FieldPurpose
ESR numberE.g. "ESR-3144"
ESR valid-to dateExpiry — triggers renewal alert 90 days before
Scope of ESRWhich use categories are covered (structural, decking, cladding, fire…)
ESR attachmentsPDF report + underlying test reports

Where it surfaces

  • Product form — dedicated "US Code Compliance" tab
  • Sales order — US customer + certification required → ESR must be present and not expired (hard block)
  • Invoice — ESR number in the footer or as a per-line note
  • Submittal PDF — generated on demand for architect / GC (not part of the invoice flow)
Governance alert. Expiry alert fires 90 days before ESR expiry. A lapsed ESR on a US-bound SO line hard-blocks shipment — this is a regulatory risk, not a commercial one.

10. FAQ

Q. Why can't I override the MO mixing block just once?

Because the claim is a chain-of-custody promise we make to the auditor. A single mixed MO contaminates every downstream lot. If you genuinely need to consume two different claims, run two MOs — that's the correct approach.

Q. What if a customer asks for FSC but I only have PEFC in stock?

Answer the customer before the SO is confirmed. The certification toggle enforces what the customer contractually required. Switching schemes mid-order is a commercial conversation, not a system override.

Q. A subcontractor returned goods with a different claim from what the inputs supported. What happens?

System auto-quarantines the output and creates a nonconformity record. Quality must investigate before the goods can be used or shipped. Typical outcomes: (a) subcontractor corrects their paperwork, (b) goods are downgraded to non-certified, (c) the deviation is logged and the subcontractor is put on watch in partner.certificate.

Q. Our ESR is expiring next month. What do I do?

You should already have been alerted 90 days out. Steps: (1) Contact ICC-ES for renewal before expiry, (2) upload the renewed ESR PDF + extend the valid-to date in Odoo, (3) if renewal slips, US-bound SO lines for the affected product will hard-block — coordinate with Sales to hold or pre-ship before expiry.

Q. Does ICC apply to European sales?

No. ICC-ES is US-specific. European customers do not ask for it, and the SO validation only triggers when the product is US-relevant (flag on the product) and the customer is US-bound.

Q. Where does the customer see all this?

Delivery slip (BOL): scheme + claim + cert code. Invoice: scheme + claim + cert code + ESR number if US. Submittal PDF (on request for large US projects): full ESR document.

Q. How long must we keep the evidence?

Five years minimum for FSC / PEFC. ESRs should be retained for the life of any building they were used in — effectively forever. Archive attachments on the lot and the product template.

11. Decisions made / pending

✅ Decided (April 2026 workshop)

  • No mixing of certificates in Kebonisation or RDK MOs — hard block, no override.
  • RDK requires both contractual clause and technical check (inputs + output reconciliation).
  • Sales order gains a certification toggle parallel to the minimum-quantity-guarantee toggle.
  • Invoice is the customer-facing proof of certification. Must carry scheme + claim + cert code per certified line.
  • ICC-ES is added as a second certification axis at product level (not lot level), US market only.

⚠ Pending
