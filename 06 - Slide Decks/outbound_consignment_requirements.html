Purpose. Operating model, data, controls, and open decisions for selling Kebony profiles through distributors on consignment. For CEO · Head of US sign-off before any code is developed. CFO is on copy — decides on financial & accounting questions (§6, Q9–Q12, Q15).
Selling entity. From July 2026, consignment sales flow through Kebony BNV. A single selling entity means no separate geographic rollout step — distributor addresses and currencies are set per agreement.

1 · Executive Summary

The CEO has directed that Kebony sell selected profiles through consignment stock held at distributor locations. The distributor holds the goods physically; Kebony retains ownership until invoice. Invoicing is triggered two ways:

  1. The distributor reports a pack sold (partial or full) → Kebony invoices the full pack immediately.
  2. If a pack remains unsold after a contractual aging period (default 90 days), Kebony auto-invoices the full pack to the distributor.

The program launches with 5 profiles and scales to more products or distributors through configuration — no code change required.

3 · End-to-End Operating Flow

3.1 Supply to distributor

  1. Internal transfer: main US warehouse → distributor consignment location.
  2. Dedicated route + picking type "Resupply Consignment at Customer" — makes all consignment moves filterable.
  3. Goods arrive at distributor — no revenue, no COGS, no accrual (internal move).
  4. Arrival date is the clock start for each lot (pack).

3.2 Sale — two trigger paths

ADistributor reports pack sold

  1. Distributor sends report (CSV or in-app wizard; a self-service portal is deferred — see §7).
  2. Kebony posts invoice for the full pack — regardless of whether distributor sold all of it.
  3. Stock moves off consignment location; DR COGS / CR Inventory, revenue recognised.

BAging trigger (automatic)

  1. Daily cron scans lots on consignment locations.
  2. If today − arrival_date > agreement.aging_days and lot not yet invoiced → auto-invoice full pack.
  3. Same accounting posting as Path A.

3.3 Revenue recognition

  • No revenue on supply transfer (internal move).
  • Revenue + COGS recognised at invoice posting (Path A or Path B).
  • All existing Kebony accrual rules apply at invoice time (royalties, mgmt fees, marketing, sales rep).

3.4 Freight capitalisation (transfer cost accounting)

Under ASC 330, freight incurred to bring inventory to its present location is capitalised into inventory value. Because Kebony retains ownership of consigned stock, transport from the US warehouse to the distributor must be capitalised, not expensed — giving COGS at invoice time the true landed cost per pack.

Mechanics reuse Odoo's landed cost pattern already in use for inbound receipts.

4.2 Stock layer

  • One stock.location per distributor address · usage = internal · x_is_consignment_out = True · linked to the agreement.
  • stock.lot gains consignment_entry_date (clock start) and consignment_invoice_id (set-once idempotency).

4.3 Invoice layer

Both Path A and Path B call one shared method _create_consignment_invoice(lot) which posts the full-pack line, sets lot.consignment_invoice_id, and triggers the standard US accrual posting.

7 · Deferred — Not Included at Launch

The items below are intentionally out of scope. Each would require a separate business case and implementation effort:

  • Distributor self-service portal — at launch, distributors report sales by CSV upload or via an in-app wizard operated by Kebony.
  • Returns / swaps of consigned packs — handled manually by the US controller at launch (credit note + manual stock reversal).
  • Automated replenishment from distributor stock levels — supply is manual (Head of US triggers transfer).
  • Consignment-specific commissions / rebates — existing Kebony accrual logic applies unchanged.
