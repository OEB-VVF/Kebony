Meeting notes:

Current CRM and Marketing Systems Overview:
Christophe, Alexandra, and George provided Rishi Pankaj with a detailed explanation of the current CRM and marketing systems in use, including Microsoft Dynamics, ActiveCampaign, SharePoint, and Salesforce, highlighting the fragmented processes and lack of integration between sales and marketing across regions.

* CRM Usage by Sales: Christophe explained that Microsoft Dynamics is used only by the sales team for managing contacts and projects, but not for leads, and access is limited to sales personnel.
* Marketing Lead Management: ActiveCampaign is used by the marketing team to communicate with prospects, mainly through email newsletters, and to track website visits for contacts, but this is not integrated with Microsoft Dynamics.
* Website Forms and Regional Differences: Website forms outside North America push leads to a SharePoint list, while in the US, leads are sent directly to Salesforce; sales receive form submissions via email and must manually decide whether to enter them into CRM.
* Fragmented Data Flow: There is no unified process for tracking leads from website visits to CRM, resulting in missed opportunities and lack of visibility on lead follow-up, as described by Christophe and confirmed by Alexandra.

Challenges and Gaps in Current Processes:

* George and Alexandra discussed the main challenges with the current CRM and marketing processes, including inconsistent CRM usage across markets, lack of harmonization, poor integration between sales and marketing, and insufficient reporting capabilities.
* Inconsistent CRM Adoption: George noted that CRM is not used effectively by all sales teams, with varying levels of discipline and two different CRM platforms in use, adding complexity and reducing efficiency.
* Lack of Integration: Alexandra highlighted that the CRM is complex for marketing to use and not harmonized across countries, making it difficult to have a unified approach.
* Missed Lead Capture: George pointed out that sample requests are not consistently entered into CRM, representing a missed opportunity for lead nurturing and follow-up.
* Reporting Deficiencies: There are no core marketing KPIs tracked in the current system, and reporting on marketing spend and effectiveness is done offline in spreadsheets, as described by George and Christophe.

Future State Requirements and Odoo Capabilities:

* Rishi Pankaj outlined the plan to develop a blueprint for an integrated CRM and marketing system using Odoo, aiming to unify lead management, improve reporting, and support both current and future website platforms, with input from the team on desired features and timelines.
* Odoo Lead Management Features: Rishi described Odoo's ability to capture leads from multiple channels, tag leads by source, and manage the entire lead-to-sale lifecycle within a single system, including integration with email and website visits.
* Website Integration Considerations: The team discussed the need to integrate Odoo with a new WordPress website, as opposed to using Odoo's native website builder, and Rishi agreed to investigate the technical requirements for this integration.
* Blueprint Development and Timeline: Rishi committed to developing a blueprint for the proposed CRM and marketing workflow within two to three weeks, aligning with the planned go-live date of July 1, and will incorporate feedback from the team.
* Access to Current Systems: Rishi requested access to ActiveCampaign and other current systems to better understand the existing setup, and Christophe agreed to provide access using an existing user account.

AI and Automation in CRM:

* Alexandra raised questions about AI features in CRM systems to support marketers, and Rishi agreed to investigate Odoo's AI capabilities for lead scoring and campaign optimization, referencing similar features in ActiveCampaign.
* AI-Driven Insights: Alexandra asked whether Odoo offers AI-driven suggestions for campaign improvement and lead identification, similar to ActiveCampaign's recommendations based on engagement metrics.
* Odoo AI Features: Rishi confirmed that Odoo includes AI-based lead scoring and probability assessments, and will further research whether it provides actionable campaign suggestions for marketers.

* Shopify Integration: Christophe explained that the US website uses Shopify to automate sample requests and shipping, and suggested exploring Odoo's e-commerce capabilities as a potential replacement.
* Coordination with US Team: Rishi was advised to connect with Hilary Barnett, the US marketing contact, to ensure the new CRM solution addresses US-specific requirements.
